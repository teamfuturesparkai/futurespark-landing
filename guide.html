What Is AI?

The Simple Explanation

AI is a computer program that learns from examples. You know how you get better at basketball the more you practice? AI works the same way — it looks at millions of examples and learns patterns. When you ask it a question or give it a task, it uses those patterns to give you an answer or create something new. It's not alive. It doesn't think like we do. But it's an incredibly powerful tool — like a supercharged calculator for words, images, and ideas.

What AI Can Do

  • Answer questions and explain topics
  • Write stories, emails, and summaries
  • Generate images from descriptions
  • Translate languages instantly
  • Help brainstorm and organize ideas
  • Analyze data and find patterns

What AI Can't Do

  • Truly understand or feel emotions
  • Always give accurate information
  • Replace human creativity and judgment
  • Know your family's unique values
  • Make ethical decisions on its own
  • Replace a great teacher or parent
"The goal isn't to compete with AI. It's to learn how to work WITH it — as a partner, a tool, and sometimes a sparring buddy for your ideas."

Your Toolkit

4 AI Tools Your Family Can Start Using Today

All of these tools have free versions. Start here — no subscriptions needed.

1

ChatGPT

Conversational AI · All ages with guidance

The most well-known AI tool. Great for answering questions, brainstorming ideas, helping with homework, and creative writing.

Try this: Ask your kid to interview ChatGPT about their favorite topic. Then ask: "Was that accurate? How would we check?"
chat.openai.com
2

Claude

Thoughtful AI for projects · Ages 10+

A thoughtful AI assistant great for research, longer projects, and careful analysis. It's what I used to build the FutureSpark AI website!

Try this: Plan a "future invention" as a family. Describe the problem it solves, and let Claude help you think through the design.
claude.ai
3

Canva AI

Design + AI images · All ages with parent guidance

A design tool with built-in AI that can generate images, create presentations, posters, and more. Visual, fun, and beginner-friendly.

Try this: Each person describes their dream bedroom, world, or superhero and generates it with AI. Print it out and hang it up!
canva.com
4

NotebookLM

Research tool by Google · Ages 12+

Upload articles, documents, or videos and get AI-powered summaries, audio overviews, and study guides. Great for research projects.

Try this: Upload an article you're all interested in. Get a summary. Then quiz each other on what you learned.
notebooklm.google.com

Weekend Activities

4 Hands-On AI Activities for Your Family

Pick one, pick two, or do all four. Each one stands alone — no specific order needed.

Activity 1
Ages 5-10 20-30 min

"AI, Tell Me a Story"

  1. Open ChatGPT or Claude with your child
  2. Together, pick a character, a setting, and a problem
  3. Ask the AI to generate a short story with those elements
  4. Read it together and ask: "What would YOU change?"
  5. Have AI revise the story based on your child's ideas
  6. Print it out or read it at bedtime
Learning moment: "Did the AI write a good story? What was missing? What would YOU have done differently?"
Activity 2
Ages 8-14 30-45 min

"Design Your Dream"

  1. Open Canva (free account)
  2. Each person picks a dream project: a bedroom, vacation poster, invention ad, or restaurant menu
  3. Use the AI image generator to create visuals for your project
  4. Spend 15 minutes designing your project
  5. Present your creation to each other
Learning moment: "How did AI help you? What did YOU add that AI couldn't have done alone?"

Weekend Activities (continued)

Activities 3 & 4

Activity 3
Ages 10-18 30-40 min

"The AI Debate"

  1. Pick a fun debate topic: Should robots have rights? Would AI make a good president? Is AI art "real" art?
  2. Split into two sides. Each side uses AI to research their position
  3. Hold a 5-minute debate — each side presents their case
  4. Discuss together: Did AI give balanced information? What was missing?
  5. Bonus: Ask AI to judge who won — then decide if you agree!
Learning moment: "AI sounds confident even when it's biased. How do we spot that?"
Activity 4
All ages 30-45 min

"Family Invention Lab"

  1. Everyone writes down one problem they wish someone would solve
  2. Vote on the best problem to tackle
  3. Ask AI to brainstorm invention ideas that could solve it
  4. As a family, pick your favorite idea and give it a name and tagline
  5. Create a 30-second "pitch" and present it — record it if you want!
Learning moment: This activity captures the essence of what FutureSpark AI teaches — using AI as a creative partner to solve real problems.
"Every one of these activities teaches something bigger than AI: collaboration, critical thinking, creativity, and the confidence to try something new."

Essential Conversations

3 AI Conversations Every Family Should Have

1
Teaching AI Literacy

"Is This True?"

AI sounds confident even when it's wrong. Practice fact-checking together. Look something up, verify it with a second source, and talk about why that matters. This one skill will serve your kids forever.

2
Understanding Ownership & Ethics

"Who Made This?"

When AI creates a poem, a picture, or a story — who's the author? Your kid? The AI? The people whose work the AI learned from? There's no single right answer, and that's exactly why it's worth discussing.

3
Setting Boundaries

"What Should AI NOT Do?"

Talk about the things we should NOT let AI decide for us. Privacy, safety, fairness, who gets a job, who goes to jail. Help your kids understand that just because AI can do something doesn't mean it should.
